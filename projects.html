Engineering Stuff

This project was focused on creating a power amplifier to connect my TV’s digital audio output to passive the speaker system, while avoiding the more costly and ugly commercial solutions. The main purpose of the power amplifier was to decode and amplify signals so they could drive multiple speakers effectively. My design can also be broaded to be used for a home theater setup, and other audio systems—such as public address setups, studio monitors, or custom multimedia projects—where converting and amplifying signals is necessary to power speakers.

Today, almost every household has a TV and a built in sound system to output sound to create sound from all around the viewer. The main purpose of this project was to connect my TV to the speaker system without having to spend a ton of money, or buy something big and ugly.

The principle behind an audio amplifier is to increase the input signal (power) so that it can drive a larger output load, which in this case is a speaker. Simplified down, an amplifier uses components like transistors or operational amplifiers to manipulate a large amoung of energy from a power supply from the inputs of a small signal

The amplifier works by taking in an input voltage, and outputing it to one input of a differential amplifier. The output of said amplifier is then scaled to a version of the original signal, determined by the gain set my external resistors, and is used to drive a load that requires more current and volatge than what the initial input signal can provide alone.

Warp Drive

The theory behind Faster-than-Light travel in Sci-Fi novels is derived from Einstein's General Relativity. The model above is an early solution proposed by physicist Miguel Alcubierre in 1994. He gave the idea of a "warp drive" that can achieve faster-than-light speeds through distorting spacetime, rather than the acceleration of the spacecraft. In his model, he describes a bubble of flat space surrounded by regions of extreme curvature, space expanding behind the bubble and contracting in front of it, which allows the space to push the bubble forwards at FTL speeds.

Miguel Alcubierre's mathematical model describes a bubble of flat space surrounded by regions of extreme curvature—space expands behind the bubble and contracts ahead of it, propelling it forward. This is the Alcubierre metric:

ds2 = -dt2 + [dx - vs(t)f(rs)dt]2 + dy2 + dz2

vs(t) is the velocity of the warp bubble, and f(rs) is a shaping function that bounds the distortion. The important thing to notice here is that the spaceship inside the bubble never exceeds the speed of light in its local frame; spacetime itself is in motion.

But the model requires "exotic matter" with negative energy density—something that contradicts general relativity's established energy conditions. The energy density is about linearly proportional to −vs2, or faster travel requires more negative energy. The Casimir effect and other phenomena suggest negative energy in bounded quantum scenarios, but producing and taming it at a useful scale remains purely theoretical.

Inside the warp bubble, the ship would experience no acceleration or time dilation, drifting effortlessly in free fall along a geodesic. That's the charm from a relativistic standpoint. But there remain intimidating challenges: the theory requires enormous amounts of energy, generates potentially catastrophic tidal forces at the edge of the bubble, and may squeeze in-falling matter to killing densities.

Alcubierre's work does not provide a buildable engine—but it broadens our thinking and makes us realize that faster-than-light travel is within the bounds of general relativity, if we can get around its physical constraints.

Einzel Lens

Einzel lenses are a type of electromagnetic lensmadeup of three aligned electrodes, with the middle electrode being held at a different electric potential than the two electrodes on the ends of the lens. This difference in electric potential creates an electric field that causes electrons passing through the lens to have their trajectory changed, resulting in the electrons focusing onto one point and creating an image. Electromagnetic lenses, like Einzel lenses, allow researchers to see into the nanoscale, far smaller than what a regular optical lens can achieve.

An Einzel lens is an electrostatic component that is commonly used to focus or collimate charged particle beams, such as electrons or ions, without altering their kinetic energy. It structurally consists of three coaxial cylindrical electrodes—two outer electrodes grounded and a middle electrode biased to a different potential (this is shown by the black rectangles, with each top and bottom pair being a coaxial cylindrical electrode). This arrangement creates a region of non-uniform electric fields that deflect the radial components of particle motion without changing their axial velocity. The electrostatic forces generated from the cylindrical symmetry and carefully controlled boundary conditions (can be seen by the blue E-field) act on particles passing through the lens, moving them toward or away from the central axis. The outcome is analogous to how optical lenses deflect light rays (the red dotted lines), and like their optical counterparts, Einzel lenses are susceptible to aberrations—such as spherical and chromatic distortions—which need to be compensated by precision engineering and voltage adjustment. Computationally, the lens action is governed by Laplace's equation in cylindrical coordinates, and simulations usually employ numerical methods to model potential distributions and particle trajectories.

Double Slit

A model of the double slit experiment, where the wave nature of light is demostrated accomplished by using a source of light but casting a light and dark fringes onto a screen, something that should be impossible if light itself was not a wave.

Young's double-slit experiment, conducted in 1801, demonstrated the wave nature of light by allowing the light to pass through two adjacent small slits onto a screen, resulting in an interference pattern of light and dark fringes due to the superposition of the light waves. This provived strong evidence that light was a wave, and not a series of particles.

Lithium Ion Battery vs Solid State Battery

The major difference between a Lithium Ion battery, the widley used batteries that we use currently, and solid state batteries lies in the state of the electrolyte used in Li-ions and solid state batteries. In a Li-ion battery, the electrolyte inside of the battery is in a liquid form, whereas in a solid state battery, the electrolyte inside is solid; this difference allows the solid state battery to be able to charge much quicker compared to a Li-ion battery, along with the benefit of being safer.
